🥐 The Croissant’s Crescent

During the 17th-century siege of Vienna, a baker named Lukas stayed up late preparing dough. One night, he heard strange noises beneath his shop—Ottoman soldiers tunneling under the city.
He warned the guards, saving Vienna from invasion.
To celebrate, Lukas baked a pastry shaped like the enemy’s crescent moon—a buttery symbol of victory. The croissant was born: flaky, golden, and defiant.
